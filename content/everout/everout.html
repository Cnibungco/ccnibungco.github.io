Everout - Native iOS mobile application for Local Seattle Events


Background & Problem

EverOut's one goal is to make it as easy as possible for you to go out and have a good time in the city you live in. Founded by the same people behind the popular events calendars at The Stranger and the Portland Mercury, EverOut.com is your one-stop shop to find out about every event and thing to do in town. We want users to be able to quickly and easily find the events you care about, and to maybe discover something new along the way.

The organization is also an active force in queer youth engagement and provides LGBTQ-centered trainings that address the needs of the most marginalized people within our community. As a queer person living in Seattle, I chose to assist Gay City due to their locality, unique place at the intersection of building queer and health education and services, and alignment of my values with theirs.

Role

I took on a UX Design role in this 2 week design sprint to design a responsive website to inform users of Gay City's resources and help them book healthcare appointments seamlessly.


Solution & Result

As a local non-profit as the website stands today, it has been long due for a revamping and has mainly be concerned with staying functional leading to a variety of user experience and overall design issues. Their main online services of informing the general public of community resources and making STI appointments were in need of a redesign.

Our goal is to know how EverOut’s current processes for searching, filtering, discovering local Seattle events and locations work for users so that we can translate it into a user-friendly and optimized native mobile application.

Empathize

I utilized both secondary research on the e-commerce retail space and one on one user interviews of frequent online shoppers to:

  1. Determine what aspects of an online store are the most helpful and important to users

  2. Understand what the average online shopper needs and wants out of their experience

  3. Understand the user flow of an average shopper and mitigate any confusion

  4. Determine if there are any affordances from the in-person shopping experience that can be easily translate online

Define - Creating a Seamless Shopping Experience

To think keep features user-centered, I articulated Problem Statements and How Might We questions (HMW) based on the research. The design was centered around helping users find quality items that is the correct size.

  1. How might we help Joshua find quality pants that fit him perfectly?

  2. How might we assist Joshua in his discovery of the latest trendy clothes?

  3. How might we make the shopping process seamless and intuitive for Joshua?

Using the findings from users interviews, I created a persona, Joshua, to keep me user-centered as I brainstormed solutions. I also created an empathy map to understand my persona - and users - in a more holistic way.

Information Architecture

I had many ideas of what could be on the website, so I narrowed down the most important features that would make a seamless online shopping experience into the project roadmap. From there, I also did an open card sort that helped me grouped features that intuitively went well together. This led to the creation of a site map. This organized categories in a clear way so that users could have an easy to navigate online shopping experience.

I spent time considering these two things, because I knew that if the website was hard to use, shoppers would be less inclined to make purchases at Knyts online. An easy to navigate website did not only benefit shoppers, it also would be highly beneficial to Knyts meeting their business goals.

A user flow helped me understand the user’s actions and decisions points as they complete a task. This also shows the number of screens that a user may encounter when completing that task.

Testing - Revisions & Reflections

Once I had my high-fidelity prototype working, I recruited 5 participants to test the prototype and observe how they completed each task. From the results and notes I gathered I created an affinity map to prioritize revisions and discover common patterns and pain points.
